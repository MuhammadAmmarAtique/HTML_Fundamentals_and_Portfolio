Histroy:

Haripur is a city located in the Hazara region of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province in Pakistan. The area has a rich history, dating back to the ancient Gandhara civilization. The name "Haripur" is derived from the words "Hari" meaning "green" and "pur" meaning "town", as the area is known for its lush green surroundings.

During the Mughal era, the region was part of the Peshawar Subah and was ruled by various Mughal governors. In the 18th century, it came under the control of the Sikh Empire and was later annexed by the British in 1849 after the Second Anglo-Sikh War.

Under British rule, Haripur became a major center for agriculture and trade. The British established a cantonment in the city and constructed a railway line, which further boosted economic activity in the area. The city also played a key role in the Indian independence movement, with many local leaders participating in the struggle for freedom.

After the partition of India in 1947, Haripur became part of Pakistan and experienced rapid development in the following years. Today, it is a bustling city with a diverse population and is known for its scenic beauty, historical sites, and thriving industries.
